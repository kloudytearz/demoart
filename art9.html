Distant View of Niagara Falls
Thomas Cole
1830
Oil on wood panel
47.9 × 60.6 cm (18 7/8 × 23 7/8 in.)

Thomas Cole's 1830 painting of Niagara Falls idealizes the American landscape, omitting industrialization while highlighting the natural grandeur. While it evokes concerns about environmental degradation, the painting also erases the impact of colonialism and presents Native American figures in a diminished, symbolic role, reinforcing the myth of the "vanishing Indian."
