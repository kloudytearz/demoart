The Watermill with the Great Red Roof
Meindert Hobbema
1660–1670
Oil on canvas
81.3 × 110 cm (32 × 43 1/4 in.); Framed: 101.3 × 130.5 × 8.3 cm (39 7/8 × 51 3/8 × 3 1/4 in.)

Meindert Hobbema, a pupil of Jacob van Ruisdael, frequently depicted watermills in his works, symbolizing both human transience and Dutch industriousness. In The Watermill, the well-dressed figures along the path represent the rewards of hard work and productivity.
