The Basket of Apples
Paul Cezanne
1887–1900
Oil on canvas
65 × 80 cm (25 7/16 × 31 1/2 in.); Framed: 88 × 104.2 × 9.6 cm (34 5/8 × 41 × 3 3/4 in.)

Paul Cézanne's The Basket of Apples defies traditional perspective, with tilted objects and sculptural folds creating a dynamic composition that focuses on underlying structure rather than realistic representation. This 1895 work, part of a significant exhibition, marked Cézanne's first public display in nearly two decades and helped solidify his reputation as a pioneer of modern art.
