The Beach at Sainte-Adresse
Claude Monet
1867
Oil on canvas
75.8 × 102.5 cm (29 13/16 × 40 5/16 in.); Framed: 104.1 × 130.2 × 11.4 cm (41 × 51 1/2 × 4 1/2 in.)

In the summer of 1867, Claude Monet painted a scene of sailboats and fishermen at Sainte-Adresse, initially starting as a depiction of yachts and tourists. First exhibited in 1876 at the second Impressionist exhibition, it was part of Monet's effort to break away from the traditional Salon system and showcase avant-garde art.
